what are the benefits?

Lowers stress and symptoms

Stress is associated with the release of many inflammatory chemicals. There has been many research that shows meditation relieves the body of stress and related symptoms.

Enhances mental health

Regular meditation can lead to higher self-confidence and a positive outlook on life, which in turn reduces the negative thoughts that can occur during daily life.

Lower blood pressure

By easing stress and the strain on your heart, meditation is known for lowering blood pressure which can also reduce the risk of a heart attack and stroke.

Increases concentration

Meditation can increase focus and the duration of the attention span, this will aid many areas of life such as work or school.

Reduce memory loss

Studies show meditation can prevent age-related memory loss. When tested those who meditate regulary have excellent mental clarity compared to others in their age bracket.

Improves sleep hygiene

If you struggle with sleep, then meditation can help you achieve deep sleep faster as it is known for easing the body and can calm those late night thoughts.

How to meditate

You may see it as just closing your eyes and concentrating on your breath, and in an essence you will be completely correct, but it can be so much harder than just that. When you first start your journey, your mind will wonder around and you will try force it back onto your breath. This can end up being quite frustrating, but take solice in the knowledge you are not alone. For most people they find guided meditation, similar to the video below, very helpful when taking their first steps. There are several different types of meditation but dont rush into them, you can't build a house without its foundations! At first just sit and listen to the guide talk you through focusing on your breath, and then letting your mind wonder and how to gently bring it back to the breath.

Where can you meditate?

Meditation can be done anywhere! Guided meditation such as the video in our how section can be replayed as many times as you need. Once you feel more comfortable in your meditation journey try put aside a few minutes of your day and start your own personal journey into meditation, you'll soon learn what works best for you and become the master of your own mind and body!
